We are relaunching Nuclear Times magazine because the next two decades must be the time when humanity ends the nuclear curse and persistent threat of annihilation. We intend Nuclear Times to be integral to organizing, reporting on, and supporting that mission.

The Deadly Truth

It turns out man was unable to safely walk the nuclear tightrope at all. In addition to the mass murder committed in Hiroshima and Nagasaki, nuclear weapons have caused millions of casualties through both short and long-term effects of nuclear testing.

Nuclear Times originally launched in 1982, at the height of the Nuclear Freeze, as the publication for the nuclear disarmament movement. We launch at a similar moment, with threats increasing, but a strong and vibrant movement growing in opposition to nuclear weapons.

This magazine will be opinionated. It will challenge the status quo. It will celebrate victories and illuminate the people on the frontlines of this movement. It will be provocative and imperfect. But mostly, it will be all of ours — the readers, writers, artists, activists dedicated to a world without nuclear weapons.

Our Mission

Like the original Nuclear Times, our intention is for the magazine to fail due to the movement's success. The end of the Cold War and the massive success of the Nuclear Freeze Movement led to the shuttering of the magazine as the 1980s came to a close.

Unfortunately, the nuclear age has not ended. We live in nuclear times and cannot simply look away, as so many have.

The 1990s did see massive reductions in warheads, decommissioning of much of the nuclear apparatus, and agreement to end open-air testing with the Comprehensive Nuclear-Test-Ban Treaty (CTBT) of 1996. All these gains are now under threat.

Three nations (India, Pakistan, North Korea) became nuclear-armed since 1998. Several nuclear treaties have been broken or abandoned and even the CTBT is on the verge of collapse. Still, we see reason for hope. More nations continue to reject nuclear weapons through the Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons.

The Time Has Come

This generation of activists face a monumental, but clear mission: to carry the memory of the hibakusha forward in a movement that rejects and dismantles nuclear weapons. To 'freeze' nuclear weapons is not enough; it's time to abolish these weapons for good.
